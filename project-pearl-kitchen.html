Project Story

Client Goals

The homeowners wanted to transform their dated galley kitchen into a modern, open-concept space that would serve as the heart of their Pearl District condo. They needed better flow for entertaining, more counter space for cooking, and a design that complemented the building's industrial character.

Design Challenges

Working within a condo meant navigating HOA regulations, managing noise for neighbors, and working with existing plumbing and electrical locations. The load-bearing wall between the kitchen and dining area required a structural beam to open up the space.

Construction Solution

Our team installed a concealed steel beam to remove the dividing wall, relocating electrical and plumbing to support the new island layout. We coordinated closely with the building management to minimize disruption to other residents.

Final Result

The finished kitchen features a 10-foot waterfall quartz island, custom walnut cabinetry, integrated appliances, and pendant lighting that highlights the open-concept design. The space now flows seamlessly into the dining and living areas.
