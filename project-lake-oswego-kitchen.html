Project Story

Client Goal

This Lake Oswego homeowner wanted to transform their outdated 1990s kitchen into a modern, open-concept space perfect for family gatherings and entertaining. The existing layout felt cramped with dark cabinetry and limited counter space, making it difficult to cook and socialize at the same time.

Design Challenges

The original kitchen in this Lake Oswego home had a peninsula wall that blocked natural light and created a closed-off feel. Plumbing for the sink needed to be relocated to accommodate the new island layout, and the existing electrical panel required an upgrade to support modern appliances and under-cabinet lighting.

Construction Process

Our team began by removing the peninsula wall to open the kitchen to the dining and living areas. We installed new plumbing lines for the island sink, upgraded the electrical panel, and ran dedicated circuits for the range, dishwasher, and refrigerator. Custom white shaker cabinetry was built to specification and installed with soft-close hardware throughout.

Materials Used

The countertops are Calacatta quartz with subtle veining, paired with a matching waterfall edge on the island. The backsplash features hand-laid subway tile in a herringbone pattern. Flooring is wide-plank white oak engineered hardwood that flows into the adjacent living spaces. All fixtures are brushed brass from Kohler.

Final Result

This kitchen remodel in Lake Oswego transformed an outdated layout into a modern open kitchen that serves as the true heart of the home. The 8-foot island provides ample prep space and seating for four, while the open layout allows natural light to flood the entire first floor. The homeowners now host regular dinner parties and love spending time in their new kitchen.
