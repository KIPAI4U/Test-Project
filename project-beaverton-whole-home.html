Project Story

Client Goal

This Beaverton family purchased a 1970s ranch home and wanted to completely modernize it while preserving the single-story livability they loved. The goal was to open up the floor plan, update every surface and system, and create a home that would serve their growing family for decades to come.

Design Challenges

The original Beaverton ranch had a compartmentalized floor plan with small rooms separated by load-bearing walls. The kitchen, dining room, and living room were all isolated from each other. Outdated plumbing, electrical, and HVAC systems needed complete replacement. The master bathroom was barely functional and the hallway bathroom served three bedrooms.

Construction Process

Our team began with a complete gut of the interior, carefully preserving the roof structure and exterior walls. Structural steel beams replaced load-bearing walls to create an open-concept living area spanning the kitchen, dining, and family room. All plumbing was replaced with PEX, the electrical system was upgraded to 200-amp service, and a new high-efficiency HVAC system was installed throughout. The master suite was reconfigured to include a walk-in closet and spa bathroom.

Materials Used

The kitchen features flat-panel walnut cabinetry with integrated handles, quartz countertops, and a large format tile backsplash. Flooring throughout is engineered white oak in a wide plank. Bathrooms feature porcelain tile, custom vanities, and frameless glass shower enclosures. Windows were replaced with energy-efficient double-pane units, and all interior doors are solid-core with modern hardware.

Final Result

This whole-home remodel in Beaverton transformed a dated 1970s ranch into a modern, light-filled family home. The open floor plan creates seamless flow between the kitchen, dining, and living areas, while the updated master suite provides a private retreat. The home now features modern systems, energy-efficient windows, and finishes that will stand the test of time. The family says it feels like a completely new home while retaining the single-story convenience they wanted.
