Project Story

Client Goal

The homeowners in this Charbonneau residence wanted to update all three bathrooms in their home — the primary bathroom, a guest bathroom, and a half bathroom — with a cohesive transitional design. The existing bathrooms hadn't been touched in years and no longer reflected the style or comfort the homeowners were looking for.

Design Approach

Working closely with the homeowners, Matt developed a unified transitional design language that would carry across all three bathrooms while giving each space its own character. The primary bathroom received the most extensive transformation, while the guest and half bathrooms were designed to complement the overall aesthetic with warm tones, clean lines, and quality fixtures throughout.

Construction Process

The renovation was phased to keep disruption manageable for the homeowners. The primary bathroom was completed first, followed by the guest bathroom, and finally the half bathroom. Each space required updated plumbing, new tile work, and fresh cabinetry. Matt coordinated the material selections so tile, hardware, and finishes tied together across all three spaces.

Final Result

The finished project brought all three bathrooms into a cohesive transitional style that feels warm, timeless, and elevated. The homeowners now have a primary bathroom that feels like a retreat, a guest bathroom their visitors love, and a half bathroom that makes a great impression. Every detail — from the tile patterns to the hardware finish — was selected with care and consistency.
