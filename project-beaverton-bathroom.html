Project Story

The Homeowner's Challenge

This Beaverton homeowner was ready to retire a primary bathroom that hadn't been updated since the home was built. The space showed its age everywhere — a green laminate countertop, worn cabinetry with doors that no longer closed properly, a cramped shower stall behind a frosted glass door, and linoleum tile flooring with a dated diamond pattern. A brass vanity light bar and small wall mirror rounded out a bathroom that felt tired and disconnected from the rest of the home.

Beyond the cosmetic issues, the layout itself needed rethinking. The shower was too small for comfortable daily use, the vanity offered limited counter space and storage, and the overall feel was dark and closed-in. The homeowners wanted a bathroom that felt modern, open, and spa-like — a true retreat at the end of the day.

The Remodeling Solution

GRIT Builders delivered this project under the Signature Bath tier — a full remodel that covers everything from demolition and waterproofing to custom tile work and fixture upgrades. Matt worked with the homeowners to develop a design that would maximize the existing footprint while completely transforming the look and feel of the space.

The old vanity was removed and replaced with custom white shaker cabinetry featuring soft-close drawers and doors with brushed nickel pulls. A polished black granite countertop with an undermount square sink replaced the worn green laminate. Above the vanity, a built-in medicine cabinet with mirrored doors was recessed into the wall between the studs, flanked by recessed LED downlights that provide even, shadow-free lighting.

The shower received the most dramatic upgrade. The cramped stall was opened up into a generous walk-in shower with a frameless glass enclosure. Floor-to-ceiling white subway tile — including a mix of flat and dimensional wavy tiles — gives the shower walls texture and visual depth. A stainless steel shower tower panel was installed with a rain head, multiple body jets, and a handheld spray, turning a basic shower into a spa-level experience. A built-in tile niche provides practical storage for bath products.

For the flooring, the homeowners chose black hexagonal tile that carries throughout the entire bathroom and into the shower, with a larger hex scale on the main floor and a smaller mosaic hex inside the shower for proper drainage slope. A teal accent wall adds a bold pop of color that contrasts beautifully with the white tile and black granite.

Project Timeline

The project was completed in six weeks — right in line with the Signature Bath timeline — with no delays or budget surprises.

  • Design & Planning: Material selections, fixture sourcing, and layout finalization
  • Demolition & Prep: Removal of all existing fixtures, tile, cabinetry, and flooring
  • Rough Work: Plumbing updates for new shower panel and vanity, electrical for recessed lighting
  • Tile & Waterproofing: Shower pan waterproofing, subway tile installation, hexagonal floor tile
  • Cabinetry & Countertops: White shaker vanity install, black granite countertop templating and installation
  • Finishing: Glass shower enclosure, fixtures, medicine cabinet, paint, and final punch list
  • Project Completion: On time and on budget

The Final Result

The completed bathroom is a complete departure from the original space. Where there was once a dark, dated room with worn finishes, there is now a bright, modern primary bathroom that feels like a spa retreat. The contrast between the white shaker cabinetry and the polished black granite countertop gives the vanity a crisp, high-end look. The subway tile shower with its stainless steel tower panel is the centerpiece of the room — a dramatic upgrade from the old frosted glass stall.

The black hexagonal floor tile ties everything together, flowing from the bathroom floor seamlessly into the shower. The teal accent wall brings warmth and personality to the space without overwhelming the clean material palette. Every detail — from the recessed medicine cabinet to the frameless glass enclosure — was selected to maximize both function and style in a bathroom that the homeowners now look forward to using every day.
